[ 01 · myth busting ]

the lies
fitness sold you.

01
"you need muscle to be a man"
You're already a man. Muscle is for you — for confidence, health, and the silhouette you want. Not for anyone else's checklist.
02
"train hard or you're not masculine"
Smart training beats hard training every time. Grinding yourself into the dirt weekly is how you get injured and quit. Progressive overload + recovery = actual results.
03
"cardio kills your gains"
Nah. Recent studies show cardio actually boosts total muscle growth when done right. Low-intensity stuff — walking, cycling, swimming — basically zero impact on your gains.
04
"eat less to lose weight faster"
Eating below ~1400 cals damages your organs. Slow cuts (0.5lb/week) preserve muscle, keep you full, and reduce loose skin. Faster ≠ better. The math just doesn't work.
05
"crunches burn belly fat"
Spot reduction is straight-up fiction. Fat loss is systemic — it comes off based on genetics and total body fat %. You can't "target" it. Build the muscle underneath instead.
06
"exercise turns fat into muscle"
These are completely different biological processes. Fat loss needs a caloric deficit. Muscle growth needs protein + stimulus. You can do both at once but they're separate systems.
07
"exercise should hurt"
Pain = injury risk, not progress. Mild burn from muscle fatigue is normal. Sharp pain, joint pain, or pain that persists? Stop. Rest. Your body isn't your enemy.
08
"you need to work out to love your body"
You don't. Exercise is a tool, not a requirement for self-worth. Do this because you want to — not because you think you owe it to anyone or because you hate what you see.

[ glossary ]
Rep
One complete motion of an exercise. 8 reps = 8 full movements.
Set
A group of reps. "3x8" = do 8 reps, rest, repeat 3 times total.
RPE
Rate of Perceived Exertion. 8 = 2 reps left. 9 = 1 rep left. 10 = dead. Aim for 8-9 most sets.
RIR
Reps In Reserve. "2 RIR" = you coulda done 2 more. Stay at 1-2 RIR most sets.
Tempo
Speed of each rep phase. "2-1-2" = 2s down, 1s pause, 2s up. Slowing down = more gains.
Concentric
The "push/pull" phase — muscle shortens. Curling up = concentric.
Eccentric
The "lowering" phase — muscle lengthens. THIS is where gains happen.
Superset
Two exercises back-to-back with zero rest. Brutal. Efficient. Great for volume.
Drop Set
Hit failure, drop weight immediately, keep going. Only on final sets.
Deload
A lighter week every 4-8 weeks. Not weakness — it's how you avoid burning out.
TDEE
Total Daily Energy Expenditure. How many calories your body burns daily.
Bulk / Cut
Bulk = small caloric surplus to grow muscle. Cut = small deficit to lose fat.

[ 03 · progression protocol ]

how to actually get stronger.

"Track. Your. Weights. I don't care if it's in your notes app, a journal, or a napkin. If you don't know what you lifted last week, you can't progress. This is non-negotiable. You're an engineer now. Engineers have data."
01
Know failure
Test your actual failure point 2-4x per month per major lift. Training within 3 reps of failure = 95–99% of max possible gains.
02
Stop 1–2 reps short
Going to complete failure every set causes excess muscle damage with barely any extra gain. Leave 1-2 in the tank on most sets. Save true failure for your last set only.
03
Increase weight → reps → sets
Hit the top of your rep range? Add 5lb. Can't add weight? Add 1-2 reps. Can't do that? Add a set for 2-4 weeks, then try again. Every session, try to do more than last time.
04
Rest times matter
Big muscles (chest, back, legs): rest 2–2.5 mins. Small muscles (arms, shoulders, calves): 1.5 mins. Rushing rest periods is one of the most common beginner mistakes.
[ 04 · hrt + exercise ]

ftm specific:
hormones + gains.

"This section exists because you deserve actual information about how T interacts with exercise, not some vague 'consult your doctor' copout. Knowledge is the whole point. Use it."

T + muscle building

Higher testosterone = more muscle protein synthesis = faster recovery = higher TDEE. If you're on T and training consistently, you're working with a hormonal environment built for this.

The baseline assumption of this program is that you're either past P3 male puberty naturally or 12+ months into HRT. If you're earlier in your transition, the program still works — progress just might look slightly different timeline-wise.

Fat redistribution

T shifts fat storage from the hips/thighs to abdominal distribution over time. Combined with resistance training building upper body mass, this creates the frame shift you're after. You cannot spot-reduce fat — but you can reduce total body fat % and build the muscle that fills your frame.

Height + frame reality check

Height matters less socially than Looksmaxing Reddit will make you think. Stocky, shorter guys exist everywhere and read male without a second thought. Your muscle fill, shoulder-to-waist ratio, and posture do more work for passing than height ever will.

Using your TDEE correctly on HRT

If you've been on HRT 6+ months: use the male TDEE calculator when estimating your caloric needs. Your metabolic rate has shifted. Running calculations based on pre-T physiology will have you eating below maintenance without realizing it.
